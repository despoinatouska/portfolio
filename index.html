About Me

I am an engineer and research enthusiast with a strong foundation in computer engineering and artificial intelligence. I hold an MSc (cum laude) in Artificial Intelligence from the University of Amsterdam and an MEng in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki.

My research centers on representation learning across computer vision and large language models, spanning topics from object- and scene-centric decomposition to large-scale generative models. I am particularly interested in physics-inspired AI and the intersection of AI and physics, exploring how physical principles can inform learning systems and how AI can advance scientific discovery.
